What is the operating ratio?
the operating ratio is a measurement of how cost-efficient a company is by comparing it's oerating costs to it's sales.
How to calculate operating ratio?
To calculate operating ratio you should use the following formula:
operating ratio = (cost of goods sold (COGS) + operating expenses) / net sales
example: company x had reported a net sales of $5,230,000 in the previous year and the cost of goods sold was %3,100,000 and the total operating expenses were $890,000, calculate the operating ratio of the company.
using the operating ratio formula the answer will be:
operating ratio = (cost of goods sold + operating expenses) / net sales
operating ratio = ($3,100,000 + $890,000) / $5,230,000
operating ratio = 76%
Why use operating ratio?
the operating ratio is used to whow how efficient a company's management is at keeping the cost low while generating revenue or sales.
